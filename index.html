Core idea

HDR as a gain-map video

Keep the SDR generator responsible for content and motion, then learn a synchronized gain-map video that expands scene radiometry.

SDR prior Freeze the pretrained video branch so structure, camera motion, and object motion stay stable.
Gain-map layer Predict standard-compatible radiometric expansion instead of replacing the generated SDR clip.
Efficient inference Token Cache reaches 3.4x theoretical speedup by refreshing only salient gain-map tokens.

Method

Shared latent, selective computation

The two branches share early denoising states so HDR appearance follows the same geometry, while cached tokens skip stable regions.

Shared-Latent Coupling Reuse early trajectories before re-noising to preserve structural alignment across branches.
Token Cache Update high-change gain-map regions and reuse cached predictions elsewhere.
HDR reconstruction Combine SDR frames and gain maps into display-ready HDR video.

Dataset and evaluation

HDRVid-18K paired video corpus

18,937 aligned SDR, HDR, gain-map clips, and captions emphasize difficult illumination: fire, stage lights, reflective metal, bright skies, and dark interiors.
